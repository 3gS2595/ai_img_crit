So far, the reactions to the reopening have been predictably either-or: MOMA is either failing the modernist past or the postmodern present; it’s being too inclusive or not inclusive enough; it’s betraying founding director Alfred Barr’s teleological torpedo or torpedoing him altogether.
In 1941, MoMA’s great founding director, Alfred Barr, famously drew a diagram of the museum’s ideal permanent collection as a torpedo.
